The two Malaysian men tried to swim to Malaysia while holding black trash bags over their heads to conceal themselves, says ICA.

SINGAPORE: Two Malaysian men were arrested on Wednesday morning (Nov 25) for trying to swim across the Causeway from Singapore, the Immigration and Checkpoints Authority (ICA) said.

The men, aged 37 and 39, tried to disguise their attempt by holding black trash bags over their heads, but officers at Woodlands Checkpoint found them near the shoreline towards Malaysia.

Preliminary investigations showed that the men had overstayed in Singapore, said ICA. The penalties for overstaying or illegal entry are up to six months' jail, as well as a minimum of three strokes of the cane. The penalty for illegal departure is a fine of up to S$2,000, a jail term of up to six months, or both, the ICA said.

Investigations are ongoing, ICA added.
